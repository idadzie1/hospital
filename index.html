Dr Asamoa Frimpong, Head of Emergency Unit, GH Hospital

The emergency department (ED) or Accident & Emergency (A&E), at GH hospital specializes in the acute care of patients who arrive without prior appointment, often via ambulance or their own means. The unit handles a wide range of medical emergencies, from minor injuries to life-threatening conditions, and are equipped to provide immediate treatment and stabilization. Key aspects include triage, where patients are assessed and prioritized, and specialized areas for different levels of care, such as resuscitation and trauma

Dr. Mustapha Isu Dadzie, Head of ICU Unit, GH Hospital

An Intensive Care Unit (ICU) at the GH hospital is a specialized hospital department providing critical care for patients with severe, life-threatening illnesses and injuries. The unit feature advanced medical equipment, a higher staff-to-patient ratio, and highly trained professionals to manage and stabilize patients with conditions requiring constant monitoring and support.

Dr Farida Isu Dadzie, Head of Surgical Unit, GH Hospital

This unit is a specialized area focused on providing care for patients before, during, and after surgical procedures. It includes the operating room (OR) where surgeries are performed, as well as pre- and post-operative care areas. Our surgical units ranges from those specializing in specific types of surgery, like cardiac or orthopedic, to general surgical units that handle a wider variety of procedures.

Dr. Memuna Isu Dadzie, Head of Obstetric Gynecology Unit, GH Hospital

The Obstetrics and Gynecology (OBGYN) unit provides specialized care for women's reproductive health, including pregnancy, childbirth, and related conditions. It combines the fields of obstetrics (care during pregnancy and childbirth) and gynecology (diagnosis and treatment of female reproductive system disorders). The unit typically offers a range of services, from routine checkups and prenatal care to managing high-risk pregnancies and performing gynecological surgeries
